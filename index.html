How can we help you?

Free open-source software

SciFabric is a free, open-source software company focused on crowdsourcing science.

PyBossa is our free software for you to build up your own crowdsourced or citizen science project just the way you want it.

Pybossa is very flexible and it will adapt to your project needs. You can add, change or remove anything to it. No templates, no impositions, PyBossa can easily be bespoken with new features, apps or anything your mind can come up with. If you have the tech requirements, PyBossa is for you.

+ info at pybossa.com

Crowdcrafting is our open platform, built on PyBossa, where anyone can create a crowdsourced or citizen science project.

It is very easy to use and requires no technical knowledge. Everything is already set up so you can focus on the important. We'll do the rest. Need specific features? We got it. At Crowdcrafting you will also have access to hundreds of volunteers who are already collaborating on projects.

+ info at crowdcrafting.org

Case Studies

People and Institutions around the world are already trusting SciFabric.

Micropasts

The British Museum and the University College of London hired SciFabric to build their own PyBossa powered citsci platform to achieve two different objectives: digitizing the huge card catalog of British prehistoric metal artifacts, and creating a high quality 3D model of every one of these archaeological artifacts via process known as photo-masking.

+ info

Cities at Night

Supported by NASA, the Complutense University of Madrid (UCM) is leading one of Crowdcrafting’s most ambitious projects to study energy use and light pollution. Their main goal is to catalog images taken by astronauts from the International Space Station: algorithms cannot distinguish between stars, cities, and other objects, and humans are much more efficient for complex image analysis.
