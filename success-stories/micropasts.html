The University College of London and British Museum project

Crowdsourcing the British Bronze Age

The MicroPasts project is run by the University College London (UCL) and British Museum in collaboration with the Institute of Archaeology, and the Arts & Humanities Research Council. It aims to explore the use of crowdsourcing in archaeological research.

Scifabric has designed two customised crowdsourcing platforms to help analyse the British Museum’s vast collection of Bronze Age artefacts. Thanks to the simplicity of PyBossa software, extensive documentation and on-going support from Scifabric, the MicroPasts team has developed more than 70 new projects.

PROJECT DEVELOPMENT

Harnessing the power of the crowd
for archaeological research

Scifabric created two templates to help MicroPasts with its research: one involving archival transcription and geo-referencing, while the other focused on photo-masking to support 3D object modelling. We’ve created platforms so anyone can easily build projects with similar functionality. Read more in our blog post

Archival transcription

Contributors transcribe index cards that describe artefacts and geo-reference their locations of discovery on an interactive map.

Photo-Masking

Volunteers accurately outline artefacts depicted in photographs so that researchers may produce high quality 3D models.

MicroPasts has so far attracted more than a thousand unique partners who have completed around 38,000 individual tasks. Anyone may contribute to the project and no prior experience is necessary.

All collected data is openly accessible and published under a Creative Commons licence. PyBossa software is also open-source and publicly available on Github.
